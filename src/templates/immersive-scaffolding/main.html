Imports

Wine

Wine is the UK’s biggest food import, totalling more than $xbn per year. Thanks to Britain’s taste for champagne, France is firmly at the top of Britain’s biggest wine importers. It’s no surprise that champagne manufacturers are concerned about an influx of ‘British champagne’ after Brexit, unless the UK signs up to protect the names of thousands of European products.

Imports

Cheese

Mozzarella, brie, feta, halloumi and hundreds of other European cheeses have become staples in British food shops, making cheese one of Britain’s biggest EU food imports. But it’s Ireland that is the unlikely top exporter of cheese to the UK, with more than $300m of Irish cheese coming into Britain each year.

Imports

Bacon and ham

Denmark’s bacon makes it the top exporter of bacon and ham to the UK, closely followed by the Netherlands, Germany and Italy.

Imports

Frozen potatoes

Frozen potato products are the largest category of UK potato imports, and 99% of those imports come from the EU.

Imports

Tomatoes

Britain imports more than $xbn of fresh tomatoes every year. Many of them come from the Netherland’s super-efficient tomato greenhouses, which produce more tomatoes per square mile than any other country in the world.

Exports

Distilled alcoholic beverages

Brexiteers insist that in the event of a no-deal Brexit, Britain wouldn’t impose import tariffs on food coming from the EU, but imports are only one side of the coin. The EU is their biggest market for thousands of British food exporters, and tariffs are an inevitability if Britain leaves without a deal. Britain’s top export is distilled alcoholic spirits – British gin and Scotch whisky, which makes up more than $2bn of EU exports every year.

Exports

Chocolate products

Exports of British chocolate to the EU have continued to grow in recent years, even with Brexit approaching. Ireland is the top destination for British chocolate exports.
